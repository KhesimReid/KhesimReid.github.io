Hobbies

When I'm not busy pursuing my university studies, I like to keep myself occupied with various hobbies.

  • Martial Arts
  • Music
  • Reading

Martial Arts have been a big part of my life for a number of years, starting from when I first began training in ITF Taekwon-Do at about seven years old. In my twelve years of training, I climbed the ranks to 2nd Dan (2nd Degree Black Belt) and travelled to Las Vegas for an International Instructors Course, where I had the opportunity to train with practitioners from around the world, under the tutilage of Masters and Grand Masters. In my time at university, I sometimes attend Jiu Jitsu and Kick Boxing sessions at the UoM MMA Society.

As a so called 'Music Junkie', I have often enjoyed both playing and listening to various forms of music. I was a member of the ALHCS Steel Orchestra, as a Lead Pan Player, and the Concert Band, as First Clarinet, for most of my high school career. I also completed the CSEC Music examination and managed to place 2nd in the entire Caribbean region. I have also done a bit of musical composition and arranging, as well as piano playing.

Even in this digital age, I still love to read books when I have the time. My favourite genres include fantasy novels like 'Harry Potter'; dystopian fiction such as 'The Hunger Games', 'Divergent' and 'Legend'; and graphic novels of Japanese origin, known as Manga. I also enjoy reading in order to enhance my knowledge base and personal development in areas unrelated to my studies. I've found books such as 'Rich Dad, Poor Dad'; Peter Thiel's 'Zero to One'; and John C. Maxwell's 'Irrefutable Laws of Leadership' to be worthwhile reads for young adults with big dreams.
